2 Assess the impacts and costs

Tip – Know what you’re in for

Consider whether you can afford the potential costs and impacts on your business, including:

  • recruitment costs
  • wages
  • allowances, overtime and penalty rates
  • tax and super
  • workers compensation insurance
  • changes to your workplace to make it accessible and safe
  • changes to the way you operate your business

Find pay rates and conditions

Must do

For most employees, you can find the minimum pay rates and conditions in the relevant award. An award is a document that sets out the minimum pay and conditions for a particular industry or occupation. Use the Find my award tool to check:

  • pay
  • hours of work
  • rosters
  • breaks
  • allowances
  • penalty rates
  • overtime

You can also use the Pay Calculator to work out what you’ll need to pay your new employee under the relevant award (including any penalty rates).

While some employees aren’t covered by an award or agreement, all employees in Australia are entitled to:

  • a minimum wage
  • 10 minimum entitlements that include maximum working hours, leave entitlements and termination requirements (10 National Employment Standards (NES))

You must meet your obligations under the relevant award and the NES. If you don’t, you may face penalties.

If you’re considering a job trial or probation period, you’ll still need to check entitlements for pay and conditions.

If you’re in WA, check for pay rates and conditions under the WA industrial relations system.


Understand potential impacts on your workplace

Must do

When you take on an employee, you’re responsible for managing them and providing them with a healthy and safe working environment. To do this, you may have to make changes to your workplace. Make sure you provide:

  • safe premises
  • safe machinery and materials
  • safe systems of work
  • information, instruction, training and supervision
  • a suitable working environment and facilities

You will also need to have worker’s compensation insurance for your employee.

If you want to learn more, check our Managing and developing staff topic.
