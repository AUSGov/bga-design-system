Style and tone

The illustration style for business.gov.au is simple and refined. Illustrations help lighten the tone of the site and make the pages feel more colourful and friendly.

Illustrations tell a positive story, rather than using negative or emotive imagery.

Style guidelines for creating illustrations

The following style rules are used to ensure a consistent look and feel across the site:

  • Illustrations are line drawings with no solid fill.
  • Illustrations have a shadow layer underneath the line drawing that contains a pale solid fill. This fill loosely indicates shadows in the drawing, it should not take up a large amount of the image.
  • Lines weights are uniform and do not vary within a lines. They are limited to a few set thicknesses, e.g. for a 440px wide hero illustration line weights are:
    • Illustration core lines: 3pts
    • Illustration background lines: 2pts
  • Line weights should scale with the illustration when displayed at a larger size.
  • Lines have rounded corners and endpoints.
  • Illustrations should not be too complicated, a few simple lines is preferred over a high-level of detail.
  • One colour per illustration. The shadow layer is generally coloured with a tint of the main colour. A range of colours from the business.gov.au palette are used for illustrations as shown below.
  • Illustrations should only display objects or situations that belong together. The background surrounding the core illustration should depict:
    • a landscape
    • objects that would naturally be seen with the core subject matter.
  • When people are included they do not have faces.

Hero illustrations

Hero illustrations are primarily used on hero pathway navigation tiles.

They are designed to be responsive to screen widths. At full width they contain a core illustration and a supporting background illustration. At smaller sizes the background image is not displayed.

Guide illustrations

Guide illustrations are used on Guide navigation tiles.

Colour use for guide illustrations is different from the rest of the site with black lines and coloured backgrounds.

Guide illustrations are not responsive - they do not appear without a supporting background.

Header illustrations

Header illustrations are used in page headers on navigation pages and guide pages. They add detail to the headers but are designed to blend into the background rather than appear as a visual feature on the page.

Header illustrations contain only a core image, they do no have a surrounding background image.
