Style and tone

The illustration style for business.gov.au is simple and refined. Illustrations are used to help lighten the tone of the site and make the pages feel more colourful and friendly.

Illustrations in the site should tell a positive story, rather than use negative or emotive imagery.

Style guidelines for creating illustrations

The following style rules must always be applied to illustrations to ensure a consistent look and feel is achieved across the site:

  • Illustrations are line drawings with no solid fill.
  • Illustrations have a ’shadow’ layer underneath the line drawing that contains a pale solid fill. This shadow loosely indicates shadows in the drawing, it should not take up a large amount of the image.
  • Lines weights are uniform and do not vary within a lines. They are limited to a few set thicknesses, e.g. for a 440px wide hero illustration line weights are:
    • Illustration core lines: 3pts
    • Illustration background lines: 2pts
  • Line weights should scale with the illustration if it is displayed at a larger size.
  • Lines have rounded corners and endpoints.
  • Illustrations should not be too complicated, a few simple lines is preferred over a high-level of detail.
  • One colour per illustration. The shadow layer is generally coloured with a tint of the main colour. A number of colours from the business.gov.au can be used for illustrations as shown below.
  • Illustrations should only display objects or situations that belong together. The background surrounding the core illustration should either depict a landscape or objects that would naturally be seen with the core subject matter.
  • When people are included they do not have faces.

Hero illustrations

Hero illustrations are primarily used on hero pathway navigation tiles.

They are designed to be responsive to screen widths. At full width they contain a core illustration and a supporting background illustration. At smaller sizes the background image is not displayed.
